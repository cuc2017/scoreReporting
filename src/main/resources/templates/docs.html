Duties

As scorekeepers you have three main duties:

  1. Keeping track of the score
  2. Game time management
  3. Keeping stats (goals and assist)

At most games there should be 2 scorekeepers so you should have someone to help.

Equipment

Everything you need to score keep should be in the scorekeeper kit. This includes:

  • Score sheets
  • Spirit score sheets
  • Pens
  • Tablet for live score keeping
  • Whistle (and wipes to clean it)
  • Stop watch
  • Rule book

You will also have a short range radio to:

  • Ask for help
  • If water is very low or out (you can also flag down a member of the field service team)
  • Seek medical or emergency assistance

Keeping Track of the Score and Stats

The official score and stats are kept on the score sheet. Please try and fill in out as accurately as possible. If you did not see who scored the goal or threw the assist, please ask the team that scored.

We are also doing live score reporting using a tablet. A test system of which can be found at Score a Game. This is a test system so feel free to try it out. You do not need to login to use the test system.

At the fields, the tablets should be logged on and ready to go. To start scoring a game select SCORE GAME from the navigation bar.

At the end of the game, ensure both captains sign the score sheet and each complete a spirit score sheet.

Game Time Management

You are acting as a timekeeper as well as a scorekeeper. You will have a whistle (there are wipes to clean it before/after you use it) and a stopwatch (or you can use the timing on the tablet). The details on time management can be found on the Scorekeepers Cheat Sheet.

If there are observers at the game, the score keepers are not responsible for time management.

Additional Notes

You are our eyes and ears on what is happening on the fields and during games. If you have any problems or concerns, please find one of the members of the local organizing committee or contact us by using the radio.
